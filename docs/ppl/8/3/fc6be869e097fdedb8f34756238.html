WOLF MARCOVICH DIES LAST NIGHT
IN SAN ANTONIO
FIRST PAGE
Wolf Marcovich, pioneer realtor and one of the foremost leaders in the development of the Indiana Harbor business sector, died suddenly last night in San Antonio, Texas. He was 68 years old.
Often referred to as the "father of Main street," Marvocich passed away after suffering a heart attack.
He was on. his annual winter sojourn and left for the lone star state two weeks ago.
He came to Indians Harbor in 1904 and participated in city politics before founding the first commercial club in the Twin Cities. Intimates had adverse opinions when he invested in a massive business structure in the wooden sidewalk downtown Harbor area His transportation and street improvement proposals became a reality through the trade association.
With wide real estate aquisitions, Marcovich handled steamship and international banking accounts. His business establishment was located at 3716 Main street and residence at 4804 Ivy street.
Surviving are the widow, Buna; daughters, Mrs. FannieTurbow of Indiana Harbor and Mrs. Sam Rea-ven of Cleveland, Ohio; sons,
Dr. A. W. Marcovich, of the Billings Memorial hospital staff; Paul Mar-covich, manager of the Euclid
